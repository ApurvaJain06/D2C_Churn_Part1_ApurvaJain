# Business Memo

## To: Product, Marketing and Customer Success Teams

### Subject: Initial Findings from Customer Churn Audit and Exploratory Analysis

A preliminary audit and exploratory analysis of customer, order, support, web activity, and campaign data was conducted to identify potential drivers of customer churn.

---

## Key Findings

### 1. Customer Engagement Appears Critical

Customers with lower website activity, fewer product views, and limited campaign interaction may represent a higher churn-risk segment.

### 2. Support Experience May Influence Retention

Customers who frequently contact support or experience service-related issues may be more likely to discontinue purchasing.

### 3. Return Behaviour Requires Investigation

Returned orders represent a potential indicator of customer dissatisfaction and should be monitored closely.

### 4. Purchase Frequency Matters

Customers with limited purchasing history may have weaker brand loyalty and may require additional engagement strategies.

### 5. Campaign Effectiveness Should Be Evaluated

The company currently uses multiple campaign types; however, customer responsiveness varies and campaign performance should be measured before expanding retention spending.

---

## Recommended Investigations

1. Analyze customer engagement decline before churn events.
2. Identify support issues most strongly associated with customer dissatisfaction.
3. Investigate product categories with elevated return rates.
4. Measure retention performance by campaign type.
5. Prioritize high-value customers showing signs of declining activity.

---

## Recommended Next Steps

Rather than applying blanket discounts to all customers, retention efforts should focus on customers exhibiting early warning signals such as declining engagement, increasing support interactions, and reduced purchasing frequency.

This targeted approach is expected to improve retention efficiency while minimizing unnecessary promotional spending.
