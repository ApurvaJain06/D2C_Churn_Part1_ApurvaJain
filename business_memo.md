# Executive Business Briefing: Customer Attrition Friction Analysis

**To**: Brand Marketing & Executive Stakeholders  
**From**: Data Intelligence Operations  

### Strategic Overview
An exploratory data evaluation shows that customer attrition patterns are strongly connected to unresolved service complaints and interaction barriers, rather than product category pricing adjustments.

### Data-Backed Risk Hypotheses
1. **The Support Escalation Wall**: Customers logging multiple support requests show a significantly higher rate of leaving, pinpointing clear friction points in resolving complaints.
2. **Value Tier Vulnerability**: Core attrition patterns are not limited to discount shoppers; established high-spending profiles show clear drop-offs when customer service inquiries stall.
3. **Product Preference Consistency**: Attrition indicators remain relatively stable across hair care and skin care categories, confirming that product formulas are not the primary driver of customer loss.

### Operational Recommendations Before Marketing Launch
* Stop broad, unsegmented promotional discount rollouts to avoid hurting profit margins.
* Prioritize resolving outstanding customer service backlogs for your highest-spending tier of accounts before executing any win-back marketing campaigns.
